# 05 - Interviews

## Purpose
Interviews were conducted to gather qualitative insights from potential real users of the BookEase system.

---

## Interview 1 – Business Owner (Salon Manager)

**Interviewee:** Nadia R. (name changed for privacy)
**Role:** Owner of a small hair salon
**Date:** Conducted informally during research phase
**Method:** In-person conversation (~15 minutes)

### Questions & Answers

**Q: How do you currently manage appointments?**
> "Mostly through WhatsApp. Customers message me and I check my notebook and confirm. Sometimes I forget to write it down and end up double-booking."

**Q: What problems does this cause?**
> "It's stressful. Sometimes two people show up at the same time and I have to apologize. I also miss messages when I'm busy with a client."

**Q: What would an ideal booking tool look like for you?**
> "Something simple where customers can pick a time themselves. I just want to see my schedule for the day clearly, and get notified when someone books."

**Q: Would you pay for such a tool?**
> "Maybe a little, but not much. I'm a small business. If it's free or cheap and it works, yes."

### Key Insights
- Double-booking is a real and frustrating problem
- Admin needs a clear daily view of appointments
- Simplicity is more important than features
- Price sensitivity is high for small businesses

---

## Interview 2 – Customer (University Student)

**Interviewee:** Rafiq M. (name changed for privacy)
**Role:** Student who frequently books tutoring and gym sessions
**Date:** Conducted informally during research phase
**Method:** Short chat conversation (~10 minutes)

### Questions & Answers

**Q: How do you usually book appointments or sessions?**
> "It depends. Some places have apps, others make me call or message. Calling is annoying because I have to wait for them to reply."

**Q: What frustrates you most about booking services?**
> "When I don't know if my booking was confirmed. I've shown up and they had no record of my appointment."

**Q: What would make your experience better?**
> "If I could just see the open slots online and pick one. And get a confirmation so I know it's locked in."

**Q: Would you create an account to use such a system?**
> "Yes, if it remembers my details and I can see my past bookings."

### Key Insights
- Confirmation is critical for customer trust
- Self-service availability view is highly desired
- Account registration is acceptable if it adds value (booking history, quick re-booking)
